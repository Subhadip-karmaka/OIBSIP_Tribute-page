Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam - 11th President of India (2002-2007)

Quick Facts

Born: 15 October 1931, Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu

Died: 27 July 2015, Shillong, Meghalaya

Fields: Aerospace Engineering, Physics

Known for: Missile Development, Nuclear Weapons Program

Awards: Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan, Bharat Ratna

About the Legend

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

Dr. Kalam was intimately involved in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He thus came to be known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He also played a pivotal organizational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.

Dr. Kalam was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour. He was a beloved figure, especially among students and youth, often referred to as the "People's President" for his humble and accessible nature.

Timeline of His Life

1931
Born on October 15 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
1955
Moved to Madras (now Chennai) to study Aerospace Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
1960
Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist.
1969
Transferred to ISRO as the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
1980s
Headed multiple nuclear tests and oversaw development of Agni and Prithvi missiles; successfully led the launch of the Rohini satellite into near-earth orbit using SLV-III.
1990
Awarded the Padma Vibhushan award, for his significant contributions to the field of science and technology.
1992-1999
Served as Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO
1998
Served as the Chief Project Coordinator for the Pokhran-II nuclear test, pivotal in establishing India as a nuclear power.
2002-2007
Served as the 11th President of India
2015
Passed away on July 27 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong
